    • I am a masters student in the Electrical Engineering Department at Stanford University studying computer systems and computer architecture. I received a Bachelor of Science in Computer Engineering and Computer Science from the University of Wisconsin-Madison (Go Badgers!).

    My masters degree is focused on conducting research over taking courses (although I do both). I conduct research under Professor Kunle Olukotun in the Pervasive Parallelism Laboratory. My current work entails creating a domain specific language for large-scale graph analytics in the Delite compiler framework. Much of this works entails developing algorithms for parallel and heterogenous architectures stemming from previous work done in Green-Marl.

    Relevant technical course work includes: computer architecture (superscalar, multi-core, SIMD), operating systems, programming languages, databases, computer graphics, digital system design and synthesis, logic design.
